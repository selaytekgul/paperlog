# Paperlog friend test

Thank you for testing an early version of Paperlog. Paperlog is a social reading diary for research papers, not a formal peer-review system. Short reactions, first impressions, questions, and personal experiences with a paper are welcome.

Use: `https://paperlog.net`

## Suggested 10–15 minute test

1. Open Paperlog and search for a paper you know.
2. Open the paper page and check whether its title, authors, year, and link look correct.
3. Sign in when you are ready to write.
4. Choose a reading status and give the paper a one-to-five-star rating.
5. Leave a short note about what you understood, valued, questioned, or experienced.
6. Edit the note once and confirm the update appears.
7. Save another paper for later or add it to a list.
8. Open a reader profile and explore one community interaction such as helpful, reply, or follow.
9. If appropriate, open a paper's reproducibility section and see whether the questions make sense.
10. Use the contact or report feature once as a test and clearly write “TEST” in the message.

Do not enter confidential research, unpublished results, private review material, passwords, payment information, health information, or anything you do not want associated with your account.

## Feedback to send the operator

- What were you trying to do?
- What page were you on?
- What did you expect?
- What happened instead?
- Which device and browser did you use?
- Could you complete the task without help?
- What felt confusing, unnecessary, or missing?
- Would you return to log a second paper? Why or why not?

A screenshot is helpful for visual problems, but remove or cover private information first.
